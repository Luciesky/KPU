Saturday, June 08

Arrival in Bogotá, general orientation at the hotel (4pm).

Sunday, June 09

Cultural tour of Bogotá: museums, old city center.
Welcome dinner.

Monday, June 10

Cultural immersion tour of Bogotá to acquire a better understanding of the city and region. Visits to universities and institutions, meetings with professors, researchers and students.

Tuesday, June 11

Cultural immersion tour of Bogotá.
Meetings and events organized according to the composition of the group.

Wednesday, June 12

Travel day: Bogotá to Leticia.
Arrival at Leticia in the early afternoon, check-in and lunch at the hotel.
Walk to the local market in Leticia, to Santander Park (parrots), followed by a group dinner.

Thursday, June 13

Meeting at the National University, which has a program of Amazonian Studies.
Lunch in Leticia.
Afternoon boat trip to Calanoa.
Welcome refreshments and guided tour of the Reserve’s fruit garden and buildings.
Explanation of the Calanoa project concepts of sustainable design.

Friday, June 14

Morning: visit to Maikuchiga, a monkey rescue centre.
Lecture by Dr. Sara Bennett.
Return by way of the village of Mocagua.
Lunch at Calanoa.

Afternoon: crossing to the Matamatá creek, at the edge of the Amacayacu Natural Park, with indigenous guides.
Explanation of the use of plants for medicinal use, crafts and building materials.
Return at dusk to Calanoa by boat, drifting down the creek and listening to the sounds of the forest.

Saturday, June 15

Morning: workshop at Calanoa. Exploration of materials and design, meetings with local crafts and arts masters.
Lunch at Calanoa.

Afternoon: visit to Mocagua Island, home to unique species of plants and birds.

Sunday, June 16

Morning: meeting with a shaman for a glimpse of an Amazonian vision of the world.

Afternoon: Boat trip to search for river dolphins by the Peruvian shore.

Monday, June 17

Amacayacu Natural Park: a (6 hour) hike from Matamatá Creek, beside Calanoa, to the Amacayacu River and San Martin de Amacayacu.
After lunch, a visit to the crop fields to explore Amazonian horticultural practices.
Return by boat to Calanoa.

Tuesday, June 18

Workshop in Calanoa with Families in Action (a mothers’ collective) and elders in the community.

Wednesday, June 19

Morning workshop in Calanoa.
Lunch and afternoon in Puerto Nariño, 20 km up river, to see an example of a sustainable Amazon village. Meeting with the authorities of the town. Return at dusk to Calanoa.

Thursday, June 20

Workshop day in Calanoa: reflection on the workshops and experiences acquired during the trip. Questions and feedback.
Evening farewell party.

Friday, June 21

Travel day: early breakfast in Calanoa, boat trip down the Amazon to Leticia, afternoon flight to Bogotá.

Saturday, June 22

Flight home

July

Classes at Kwantlen: debriefing, discussions, presentations.

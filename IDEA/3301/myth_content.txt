
Content

An introduction to myths as carriers of cultural knowledge
Myths as versions of truth and as effective containers for sacred, social, political, or scientific information
Myth-making as a fundamental and necessary function of human nature
The persistence of the myth-making function and its role in the contemporary world
Mythological world views, including spirituality, mythological concepts, and approaches to the imagination
The necessity of myths and the development of new myths
Exploration and application of mythopoetic creative strategies
Future directions of myth in literature, culture, and human nature
